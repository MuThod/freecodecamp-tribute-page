Tim Berners Lee

The man who connected the World

Biography

1955
Born in London.
1976
Graduate from the University of Oxford.
1980
Work at CERN where he starts to develop the hypertext
1984
Return to CERN.
1989
Wrote a proposition to use hypertext over Internet, which was accepted. The World Wide Web was born.
1990
Publish the first Web site.
1991
Develop the softwares for the first Web server and the first Web browser.
1994
Found the W3C at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology.
2001
Become a patron of the East Dorset Heritage Trust.
2001
Accept a chair in computer science at the School of Electronics and Computer Science of the University of Southampton, Hampshire.
2004
Knighted by Queen Elizabeth II.
2007
Appointed to the Order of Merit.
2009
In a Time's article, admit that the initial pair of slashes ("//") in a web address were "unnecessary".
2013
Awarded the inaugural Queen Elizabeth Prize for Engineering.
2016
Received the ACM Turing Award "for inventing the World Wide Web, the first web browser, and the fundamental protocols and algorithms allowing the Web to scale".

Quote

He wove the World Wide Web and created a mass medium for the 21st century. The World Wide Web is Berners-Lee's alone. He designed it. He loosed it on the world. And he more than anyone else has fought to keep it open, nonproprietary and free.

Time magazine, Time 100: The Most Important People of the Century
